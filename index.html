Remember the good old days? When slipping coin after coin into your favourite video game was considered an awful waste of time and money by your parents? When pinball parlours were not regularly frequented by drug dealers and paedophiles and you could spend a whole afternoon playing and watching others? When all that mattered in your life was reaching Earth on Gyruss, achieving the high score on Kiss pinball or rescuing that same stupid bloody woman every time in Donkey Kong or Dragon's Lair?

I discovered M.A.M.E through a friend about 3 years ago, and have been having a blast with it and other arcade emulators ever since. I only recently discovered the 'Build Your Own Arcade Controls' website, and I was blown away and inspired by the idea of building a dedicated cabinet to relive the good old days. I HAD TO HAVE ONE!!

Three months of planning, three months of construction, plenty of setbacks and heartache, but at last it's finally finished! It's great to sit back in the comfort of my own home now and play all of the old classics as they were originally intended to be played (that is, when I can kick my wife off it - she loves Tetris!).
